Problem Statement

Raspberry Piâ€“based systems often require uninterrupted power for reliable operation. Sudden loss of external power can cause abrupt shutdowns, leading to data corruption or hardware instability. Hence, an automatic power selection mechanism is required to switch between an external supply and a battery source safely.

Objectives

  • Design an automatic power source selection circuit using Schottky diodes
  • Ensure uninterrupted 5 V power supply to the Raspberry Pi
  • Prevent reverse current flow into the battery

Methodology

The system uses a Schottky diode OR-ing technique where two regulated 5 V sourcesâ€”external power and a battery-driven buck converterâ€”are connected through Schottky diodes. The diode connected to the higher voltage source conducts while the other blocks current, enabling automatic switching without control logic. Output capacitors stabilize the voltage during load changes.

Diode Forward Voltage Drop: \( V_f \approx 0.2-0.3 \) V for Schottky
Power Selection: Higher \( V_{in} \) conducts, lower blocks.

Tools / Hardware Used

  • Raspberry Pi
  • 11.1 V (3S) Li-ion/Li-Po battery
  • Buck converter (11.1 V to 5.2 V)
  • Schottky diodes (SS34 / 1N5822 / 1N5819)
  • Capacitors (1000 ÂµF electrolytic, 100 nF ceramic)
  • USB-A female connector and USB-A to USB-C cable
  • Digital multimeter

Outcome / Result

The circuit successfully provided seamless power switching between the external supply and the battery. Reverse current flow was prevented, and a stable 5 V supply was maintained at the Raspberry Pi input without system reboot.

Conclusion

The Schottky diode OR circuit offers a simple, reliable, and safe solution for automatic power source selection. It eliminates complexity while ensuring system stability, making it suitable for embedded and robotics applications.
